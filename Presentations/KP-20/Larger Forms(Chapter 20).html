Form

The way a composition is structured to create a meaningful musical experience

Formal Terminology

Previously, in Chapter 10, we looked at how to classify period forms, such as:

  • Phrase - a relatively independent musical idea that ends with a cadence
  • Period - a group of two phrases in an antecedent-consequent relationship.
    • Parallel Period - both phrases begin with similar or identical material, but end differently.
    • Contrasting Period - phrase beginnings differ
  • Double Period - consists typically of four phrases in two pairs

Binary Forms

  • A form that consists of two approximately equivalent sections.

  • Sectional Binary - first part ends with tonic.

  • Continuous Binary - first part ends something other than tonic.

Ternary Form

The idea of an ABA musical structure is an important one in musical form.

  • Ternary Form - a three part form that is structured as statement-contrast-return.

  • Anything from a short melody to a lengthy movement from a symphony can be structured as a ternary form.

Haydn: Piano Sonata no. 11, III

Sonata Form

A type of ternary form that is usually found as the first movement of a sonata, string quartet, symphony, or similar work.

  1. Exposition - presents the important themes as wells as the tonal conflict between the two most important keys in the piece
  2. Development - has a more variable organization: It may develop motives from the expostion. It is unstable and often full of sequences
  3. Recapitulation - replays the piece's important themes. It does not modulate to a new key like the exposition. It stays in the primary key.

Mozart: Piano Sonata No. 7 in C, K. 309
