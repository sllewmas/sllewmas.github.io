Make a budget

Now that you have your idea, you need to determine what it will actually cost to pull off.

  • Make a spreadsheet.
  • Put everything that you can think of into the spreadsheet, and estimate the costs.
  • This is not going to be a final budget, but it will start you on the process of figuring out what finances may look like for your project.

Immediate Community

It may be difficult to solicit gifts for new projects.

The first place to look for support is in the community in which the project will impact.

Focus on building relationships that are mutually beneficial.

Immediate Community

  • Look at the budget you made, and target categories that you can address at no cost.
  • We need a venue, but we don't have room in the budget to rent a space.
  • Partner with a newly opened brewery. We get free space for the concert and bring in an eager audience interested in beer.
  • They sell a lot of beer at the show, but, more importantly, they turned the new customers onto their brand.

Immediate Community

This focus on immediate relationships will help solidify your project's role in the community.

Build a track record of activity that is centered on your project's identity.

This will help you demonstrate your project's capacity in future fundraising efforts.

Taking Donations

You can accept gifts as an individual.

If the money received is a gift, you do not need to report it as income.

Sales (tickets, CDs, swag, etc.) proceedes are not considered gifts.

An individual can give another individual up to $14,000/year before incurring any gift tax. The giver is responisble for the tax burden.

If you are dealing with that kind of charitable income, you really should be trying to gain tax-exempt status.

Crowd Funding

Pros Cons

Effective way to quickly raise a sizable sum of money.

Does not encourage the development of long-term relationships with the project. Donations are not tax-deductabile.

Helps awareness of your project online.

Campaigns are the most effective when happen once or infrequently.

Fees!

Crowd Funding Fees

  • Make sure you know what fees each crowd funding service charges.
  • Additionally, the money you raise through crowd funding will be considered personal income, and you will pay tax on it.
  • Make sure to include both fees and the estimated tax burden into your project budget.

Becoming a charitable organization

A 501(c)(3) organization is a corporation or other type of organization that is exempt from federal income tax under section 501(c)(3) of Title 26 of the United States Tax Code.

These charitable organizations can accept donations that are tax-deductible.

Gaining 501(c)(3) status varies from state to state, and can be a long and cumbersome process.

Fiscal Sponsorship

Fiscal Sponsorship: a non-profit organization offering their legal and tax-exempt status to a group, or project, engaged in activities related to the sponsoring organization's mission.

There are many different "models" of fiscal sponsorship. Likely, the most relevent to you is Model C.

Model C Fiscal Sponsorship

Your project receives grants from the fiscal sponsor.

In this model, the sponsor receives the funds that you raised and grants them to you.

It is important that the sponsor act as more than just a intermediary.

Model C Fiscal Sponsorship

You need to have an extremely detailed fiscal sponsorship agreement that outlines what fees the sponsor charges and a clear exit strategy

Use a lawyer. There are many organizations that provide free legal services to artists.
