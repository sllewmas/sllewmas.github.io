Introduction

  • Much music of the post-Romantic period remained tonal enough that we can use traditional methods of analysis.
  • However, some composers were creating music that resists any application of traditional harmonic theory.
  • Post-tonal theory provides us with a systematic way to describe the underlying structure of this music.
  • This lecture covers just some basic aspects of atonal and twelve-tone theory, including total serialization.

Basic Atonal Theory

  • Traditional analytical techniques did little to uncover the underlying structure of this music.
  • A new vocabulary was especially critical if these new pitch structures were going to be able to describe in a systematic.
  • Allen Forte first codified the techniques we now use for analyzing atonal music.
  • Set Theory - A general term used to describe methods of analyzing atonal music.

Set Theory

  • Pitch Class - Term used to group together all pitches that have an identical sound or that are identical except for the octave or octaves that separate them
  • Segmentation - The process of partitioning a piece of atonal music into pitch class sets based on relationships formed by musical parameters such as:
    • pitch rhythm phrasing
    register timbre
  • Instead, of breaking a piece up into chords, we will break it up in into pitch class sets (pc sets)

Integer Notation

  • Reinterpret the four pc sets that occur in the Schoenberg example:
    1. (B,G♯,G) → (11,8,7)
    2. (G♭,F,B) → (6,5,11)
    3. (B♭,A,D♭) → (10,9,1)
    4. (D♭,E,F) → (1,4,5)
  • In common-practice music, we could easily identify triads and seventh chords using names like major, minor, etc.
  • In atonal music, however, there frequently is no referential scale, and any combination of the 12 pitch classes is possible.

Normal Form

  • We want to figure our the quality of a pc set.
  • To do this we need to rearrange the pc set in to normal form
  • Normal Form - the most compact arrangement of a pc set, listed from left to right in ascending order within an octave.

Normal Form

[7,8,11]

Prime Form

  • To find the prime form of a pc:
    1. Find the normal form of the pc
    2. Subtract the left most number from each value
  • [7,8,11]
  • -[7,7,7]
  • (0 1 4)

Think: A 3-note chord that has a root, a note 1 half-step above the root, and another note 4 half-steps above the root.

Twelve-Tone Serialism

Twelve-Tone Serialism

  • The twelve-tone method is perhaps the most methodically revolutionary compositional technique of the post-tonal era.
  • Arnold Schoenberg is generally credited with developing and codifying this method.

Twelve-Tone Serialism

  • Why?
    • Schoenberg viewed himself as a composer in direct lineage with Bach, Mozart, Beethoven, Wagner, and Mahler.
    • All of the above wrote large scale works whose form is defined by harmonic movement.
    • Atonal music lacks the harmonic push and pull of Tonal music.
    • Schoenberg believed the 12-tone method would more systematically facilitate the composition of large-scale forms.

Twelve-Tone Serialism

  • Twelve-tone Method - method designed to equalize all 12 pitch classes.
  • Here's how:
    1. A twelve-tone composition is to be based on a series or row: an arbitrary ordering of the 12 pitch classes that is determined by the composer.
    2. No pitch class may be used again until all other pitch classes have been sounded.
    3. The series may be used in transposition, retrograde, inversion, or retrograde inversion.
